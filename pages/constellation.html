Constellations are regions of the night sky defined by stars that form a particular subject, be it an animal, a human, or an inanimate object. Different cultures have created their own constellations out of the stars, usually based off of their own mythologies. In modern times, the International Astronomical Union (IAU) divides the celestial sphere into 88 constellations, based off of Western cultural interpretations, which include the traditional 48 Greek constellations and several new additions and one partition. Notable constellations are the twelve of the zodiac, which is the region around the ecliptic. Constellations help identify features in the sky, like stars, galaxies, and nebulae. Apart from constellations, there are also small groups of stars known as asterisms.
