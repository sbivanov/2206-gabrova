The Sun, a G-type star

A star is a celestial body that consists of plasma held by it's gravity and emits light. The Sun is the closest star to Earth. There are an estimated 10^22 to 10^24 stars in the universe, but only around 4,000 can be seen with the naked eye.

Stars are formed when a nebula collapses by it's own gravity. During it's life it fuses hydrogen into helium and eventually dies, forming a white dwarf, a neutron star or a black hole, depending on a star's mass.

Classification

Stars are classified based on their spectral characteristics. In order from hottest to coldest, they are: O, B, A, F, G, K and M. The Sun is a G-type star. The most common stars are M stars, making up around 75% of all main-sequence stars, while O stars are less than one in a million.
