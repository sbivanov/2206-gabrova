Annefrank

5535 Annefrank is an inner main belt asteroid, part of the Flora family. It was discovered in 1942 . Annefrank was a secondary target of the Stardust mission that later visited comet Wild 2.

Discovey

Annefrank was discovered in 1942 by German astronomer Karl Reinmuth at the Heidelberg Observatory in Germany.

Name

Annefrank is named after Anne Frank, a victim of the Holocaust, known for her diary that documented her everyday life during World War II. The name was proposed on the fiftieth anniversary of the end of WWII.

Orbit and rotation

Annefrank is located in the inner asteroid belt. The semi-major axis is 2.21 AU, the perihelion is 2.07 AU, and the aphelion is 2.35 AU. It takes Annefrank 3.29 years to complete one orbit. The eccentricity is 0.06 and the inclination is 4.25°. The rotational period is around 15.12 hours.

Annefrank is a member of the Flora family, which comprises 13 000 asteroids.

Physical characteristics

Shape and size

Annefrank has triaxial dimensions of 6.6 x 5.0 x 3.4 km and a mean 4.3 km diameter. The shape of Annefrank is elongated and triangular, with one large impact crater.

Structure

Annefrank, like other Flora family asteroids, is part of the S spectral type, consisting of silicates.

One large impact crater can be seen on the surface of Annefrank. It has not been named.

Exploration

In 2002 Stardust flew by Annefrank at a range of about 3,078 km. During the encounter, the spacecraft’s dust collectors collected samples while its camera returned 72 images. This encounter was used as an engineering test of the spacecraft. Stardust then continued on its way to comet 81P/Wild.
