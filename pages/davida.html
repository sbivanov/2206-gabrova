Davida. Source, modified

511 Davida is an outer main belt asteroid. It was discovered in 1903. Davida is one of the largest asteroids, having a mean diameter greater than 200 km.

Discovey

Davida was discovered in 1903 by R. S. Dugan at the Heidelberg Observatory.

Name

Davida is named after David Peck Todd, an astronomy professor at Amherst College, Massachusetts, USA.

Orbit and rotation

Davida is an asteroid in the outer asteroid belt. The semi-major axis is 3.163 AU, the perihelion is 2.569 AU, and the aphelion is 3.759 AU. It takes Davida 5.626 years to complete one orbit. The eccentricity is 0.188 and the inclination is 15.94°. The rotational period of Davida is 5.13 hours.

Davida is part of the Meliboea family that consists of 444 known members, and is its largest member.

Physical characteristics

Shape and size

Davida has triaxial dimensions of 357 × 294 × 231 km and a mean 298 km diameter. Its shape is roughly ellipsoidal, but still irregular.

Structure

Davida is a C-type asteroid, consisting of carbonaceous material. Analogous to its surface are carbonaceous chondrites.

The surface of Davida has been resolved by Earth-based telescopes.

Exploration

Davida has not been visited by any spacecraft.
