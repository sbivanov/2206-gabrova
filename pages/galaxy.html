A galaxy is a system consisting of gas, dust, dark matter, stars, star remnants and other celestial bodies bound together by gravity. The name "galaxy" is derived from the Greek word for "milky" (γαλαξίας), refering to the Milky Way galaxy. Dark matter comprises the majority of the mass of a galaxy and only a few per cent of the mass is visible. Supermassive black holes are the common centerpiece of a galaxy.
