Euphrosyne. Source, modified

31 Euphrosyne is an outer main belt asteroid. It was discovered in 1854. Euphrosyne is one of the largest asteroids, having a mean diameter greater than 200 km. One natural satellite has been discovered around it.

Discovey

Euphrosyne was discovered in 1854 by James Ferguson at Washington. This is the first asteroid to be discovered in the Americas.

Name

Euphrosyne is named after one of the Charites in Greek mythology.

Orbit and rotation

Euphrosyne is an asteroid in the outer asteroid belt. The semi-major axis is 3.155 AU, the perihelion is 2.459 AU, and the aphelion is 3.852 AU. It takes Euphrosyne 5.61 years to complete one orbit. The eccentricity is 0.221 and the inclination is 26.30°. The rotational period of Euphrosyne is 5.53 hours.

Euphrosyne is part of a family that consists of about 2 000 known members. One natural satellite has been discovered, measuring around 4 km in diameter and is likely also the result of the same collision.

Physical characteristics

Shape and size

Euphrosyne has triaxial dimensions of 294 × 280 × 248 km and a mean 268 km diameter.

Structure

Euphrosyne is a C-type asteroid, consisting of carbonaceous material. Density measurements (1.7 g/cm^3) enabled by the moon of Euphrosyne show that the composition is also half ice.

The surface of Euphrosyne has been resolved by Earth-based telescopes. No craters larger than 40 km have been discovered on its surface.

Exploration

Euphrosyne has not been visited by any spacecraft.
