Eunomia. Source, modified

15 Eunomia is an intermediate main belt asteroid. It was discovered in 1851 by Annibale de Gasparis. Eunomia is one of the largest asteroids, having a mean diameter greater than 200 km.

Discovey

Eunomia was discovered in 1851 by Annibale de Gasparis at the Naples Observatory.

Name

Eunomia is named after the Greek goddess of law and order, one of the Horae.

Orbit and rotation

Eunomia is an asteroid in the intermediate asteroid belt. The semi-major axis is 2.644 AU, the perihelion is 2.15 AU, and the aphelion is 3.14 AU. It takes Eunomia 4.30 years to complete one orbit. The eccentricity is 0.186 and the inclination is 11.75°. The rotational period of Eunomia is 6.08 hours. Eunomia is in 7:16 mean-motion resonance with the planet Mars.

Eunomia is the eponym of a family that consists of 5 670 known members.

Physical characteristics

Shape and size

Eunomia has triaxial dimensions of 357 × 255 × 212 km and a mean diameter of 270 km. It has an elongated, but fairly rounded shape.

Structure

Eunomia is an S-type asteroid, consisting of silicates and some nickel-iron. Spectroscopic analysis has also shown the presence of calcium-rich pyroxenes and olivine. The surface has regions with differing compositions. This suggests that Eunomia's parent body was subject to internal heating.

The surface of Eunomia has been resolved by Earth-based telescopes.

Exploration

Eunomia has not been visited by any spacecraft.
