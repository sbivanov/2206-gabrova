Europa. Source, modified

For Jupiter's moon of the same name, see Europa

52 Europa is an outer main belt asteroid. It was discovered in 1858. Europa is one of the largest asteroids, having a mean diameter greater than 300 km.

Discovey

Europa was discovered in 1858 by Hermann Goldschmidt in Paris.

Name

Europa is named after Europa from Greek mythology. It shares the same name with the moon of Jupiter Europa

Orbit and rotation

Europa is an asteroid in the outer asteroid belt. The semi-major axis is 3.10 AU, the perihelion is 2.75 AU, and the aphelion is 3.44 AU. It takes Europa 5.5 years to complete one orbit. The eccentricity is 0.11 and the inclination is 7.48°. The rotational period of Europa is 5.6 hours.

Physical characteristics

Shape and size

Europa has triaxial dimensions of 379×330×249 km and a mean 319 km diameter, making it the sixth largest asteroid. As one of the largest asteroids, it is roughly round, close to an ellipsoid, but still irregular.

Structure

Europa is a dark C-type asteroid, consisting of carbonaceous material and silicates. Spectroscopic studies have found evidence of olivines and pyroxenes on the surface.

The surface of Europa has been resolved by Earth-based telescopes, but no significant features have been found.

Exploration

Europa has not been visited by any spacecraft.
