Interamnia. Source, modified

704 Interamnia is an outer main belt asteroid. It was discovered in 1910. Interamnia is one of the largest asteroids, having a mean diameter greater than 300 km.

Discovey

Interamnia was discovered in 1910 by Vincenzo Cerulli in Teramo, Italy.

Name

Interamnia is named after the Latin name for Teramo, Italy, where Cerulli discovered the asteroid.

Orbit and rotation

Interamnia is an asteroid in the outer asteroid belt. The semi-major axis is 3.06 AU, the perihelion is 2.58 AU, and the aphelion is 3.53 AU. It takes Interamnia 5.34 years to complete one orbit. The eccentricity is 0.155 and the inclination is 17.31°. The rotational period of Interamnia is 8.71 hours.

Physical characteristics

Despite being the fifth largest asteroid, Interamnia hasn't been well-studied, in part due to it being relatively distant.

Shape and size

Interamnia has triaxial dimensions of 362 × 348 × 310 km and a mean 332 km diameter. This makes it the fifth largest asteroid. Its shape is similar to that of Vesta, close to an ellipsoid.

Structure

Interamnia is an F-type asteroid, consisting of carbonaceous material, but with less hydrated silicate minerals. F-type asteroids are related to the C and B types.

The surface of Interamnia has been resolved by Earth-based telescopes like the Very Large Telescope. However, no large impact basins have been found.

Exploration

Interamnia has not been visited by any spacecraft.
