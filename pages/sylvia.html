Sylvia. Source, modified

87 Sylvia is an outer main belt asteroid, part of the Cybele group. It was discovered in 1866. Sylvia is one of the largest asteroids, having a mean diameter greater than 200 km. Two moons were discovered around Sylvia and are named Romulus and Remus, making it the first asteroid with multiple satellites.

Discovey

Sylvia was discovered in 1866 by Norman Robert Pogson at Madras (now Chennai), India.

Name

Sylvia is named after Rhea Silvia from Roman mythology, the mother of the brothers Romulus and Remus..

Orbit and rotation

Sylvia is an asteroid in the outer asteroid belt. The semi-major axis is 3.48 AU, the perihelion is 3.15 AU, and the aphelion is 3.81 AU. It takes Sylvia 6.5 years to complete one orbit. The eccentricity is 0.094 and the inclination is 10.9°. The rotational period of Sylvia is 5.18 hours.

Sylvia is part of the Cybele group, located in the outermost regions of the asteroid belt. Furthermore, it is also the parent body of the Sylvia family, consisting of 191 known members.

Physical characteristics

Shape and size

Sylvia has triaxial dimensions of 363 × 249 × 191 km and a mean 271 km diameter. It has an elongated shape.

Structure

Sylvia is an X-type asteroid according to the SMASS classification.

The surface of Sylvia has not been sufficiently resolved by Earth-based telescopes.

Moons

Two moons were discovered around Sylvia and are named Romulus and Remus, making it the first asteroid with multiple satellites. They are most likely a result from the same collision that has created the Sylvia family. Remus is the smaller of the two and orbits closer, with a 10 km radius, while the larger Romulus has a diameter of 27,1 km.

Exploration

Sylvia has not been visited by any spacecraft.
